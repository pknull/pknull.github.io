# The Hush

A cosmic horror novel in long-form revision. Aldric Whitcomb leaves a cabin in Lead, Wyoming for Harvard, the Florida Keys, New Orleans, and a town called Penumbra in the Laramie range. Reality stays mundane. His experience of it becomes wrong.

Six acts, seventy-four sections, written out of chronological order — the opening is the ending. *House of Leaves* territory, not sloppy editing.

---

## What it's named for

The Hush is, in-world, the Matriarch's living reach — a sedative current that dissolves fear and resistance. Not oppressive silence but purposeful quieting that cradles the mind. The book is named for a thing that wants you not to struggle.

## Where it's at

Currently in revision. The structural pass is done — flat causality is locked, the beat map is solid, every section carries embedded scaffolding (beats, voice territory, revision tier, known-issues watchlist). The work now is line-level: voice variance, perplexity scans, the slow craft pass that drags a draft from *structurally sound* to *alive on the page*.

## What it's not

Not fantasy. There is no system. No soft magic, no hard magic, no consistent rules — only a vessel and the things that work upon it. Trust implication over exposition. The horror is grounded; the spectacular is suspect.

## What it draws on

VanderMeer for biological horror as structural spine. Lovecraft for the vocabulary, filtered through everyone else rather than pastiche. Barker for flesh as text being rewritten. Aickman for atmosphere over monster. Ligotti for consciousness as burden. Plus seven more.

The territory system maps the alchemical onto the geographic — cabin marked 🜍, journey 🜂, Iris's POV 🜄, cosmic transmissions 🜒, visionary moments 🝊, magus scenes 🜞. Each section's symbols tell you where in the ritual timeline you've landed.
